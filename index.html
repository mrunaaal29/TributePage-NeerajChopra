Background

Neeraj Chopra was born on December 24, 1997, in Khandra village, Panipat district, Haryana. Raised in a humble farming family, he struggled with childhood obesity, which led his parents to encourage physical activity. At the age of 11, he was introduced to javelin throw while visiting a local stadium. What began as a way to stay fit soon became a deep passion, and his natural talent was quickly recognized by coaches who encouraged him to pursue the sport seriously.

With focused training and an unrelenting work ethic, Neeraj rapidly progressed through junior-level competitions, setting records and earning acclaim. His breakthrough came when he won a gold medal at the 2016 IAAF World U20 Championships, becoming the first Indian athlete to set a world record in javelin. This victory marked the start of his international career and established him as a rising star in Indian athletics.

Neeraj’s greatest triumph came at the 2020 Tokyo Olympics (held in 2021 due to the pandemic), where he made history by winning the gold medal in men’s javelin throw — India's first Olympic gold in athletics. His victory inspired millions across the country and elevated him to national hero status. Since then, he has continued to dominate the sport, winning medals at the World Championships and Diamond League, all while serving as a Junior Commissioned Officer (JCO) in the Indian Army.

About Javelin Throw

The javelin throw is one of the oldest and most spectacular events in athletics, combining speed, strength, and perfect technique. The goal is simple yet powerful — to throw a long spear-like object called a javelin as far as possible within a defined area. While it may look straightforward, mastering the javelin requires years of discipline, explosive power, and pinpoint precision.

Athletes sprint down a runway and launch the javelin into the air, aiming for maximum distance and stability during flight. The javelin itself is a finely engineered piece of equipment — usually around 2.6 meters long and weighing 800 grams for men. The technique involves a blend of biomechanics and art, where even a slight change in angle or grip can make a big difference in distance.

Javelin throw has been a part of the modern Olympic Games since 1908 and holds a special place in track and field. For India, it became a household name when Neeraj Chopra made history by winning the gold medal at the Tokyo 2020 Olympics. His success didn’t just break records — it inspired a whole new generation of young Indians to take interest in this thrilling sport.
